Corvus Zip Tie Installer

Mechanically Powered Wiring Harness Tool

Project Overview

The Corvus Zip Tie Installer is a fully mechanical tool designed to automate the application of zip ties onto aerospace wiring harnesses. Developed for use in microgravity training environments like the NASA Neutral Buoyancy Laboratory (NBL), the device streamlines cable bundling without relying on electrical systems — making it safe, robust, and ideal for harsh or constrained environments.


My Role

I led the mechanical design process, creating multiple prototypes using CAD tools and iterative testing. I designed and tested the alignment features and deployment actuation system to ensure smooth and consistent zip tie placement along complex harness geometries. My responsibilities also included physical testing in simulated environments and integration with astronaut tool handling protocols.


Technologies & Tools Used

  • SolidWorks for mechanical modeling and simulation
  • 3D printing for prototyping
  • Mechanical-only actuation systems
  • Human factors and ergonomic testing
