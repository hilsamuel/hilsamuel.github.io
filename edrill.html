E-Drill CRADA

Fastener Removal Tooling for F119 Nozzles

Project Overview

As part of a Cooperative Research and Development Agreement (CRADA) with Tinker Air Force Base, this project focused on improving the use of the E-Drill tool for removing titanium rivets from F119 engine nozzles. The E-Drill is an electric discharge tool that automates the drilling process, reducing technician fatigue and damage to surrounding materials.


My Role

I contributed to the mechanical design and testing of a simplified locator fixture that guides the E-Drill for optimal alignment and depth control. My work emphasized reducing technician strain, improving hole consistency, and enabling faster operation in confined engine spaces.


Technologies & Tools Used

  • SolidWorks CAD modeling
  • Rapid prototyping and fitment testing
  • Electric discharge machining principles
  • Ergonomic tooling design
